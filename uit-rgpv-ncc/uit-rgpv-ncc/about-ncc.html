National Cadet Corps (NCC)

History of NCC

The National Cadet Corps (NCC) was established in India on 16th July 1948 through the NCC Act of the Indian Parliament. It was inspired by the University Corps formed during British rule, aiming to make military training accessible to students. After independence, the need for a strong, youth-focused organization to instill patriotism, discipline, and leadership was recognized, and NCC was born under the Ministry of Defence. Today, it stands as the largest uniformed youth organization in the world, shaping countless cadets into responsible citizens and future leaders.

Objectives of NCC

  • Develop character, comradeship, discipline, leadership, and a secular outlook among youth.
  • Instill a sense of responsibility and encourage participation in nation-building.
  • Promote physical fitness and endurance through regular training and outdoor activities.
  • Foster the spirit of adventure and sportsmanship.
  • Encourage the cadets to participate in social service and community development programs.

  • A significant number of NCC alumni have gone on to serve in the Indian Armed Forces as officers and soldiers.

Cadet Pledge

We the cadets of the National Cadet Corps,
do solemnly pledge that we shall always uphold the unity of India.

We resolve to be disciplined and responsible citizens of our nation.

We shall undertake positive community service in the spirit of selflessness
and concern for our fellow beings.
